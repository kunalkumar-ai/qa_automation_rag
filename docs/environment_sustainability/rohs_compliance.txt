Subject: Compliance with RoHS 3 Directive (EU 2015/863)  

As part of ElectroTech's ongoing commitment to environmental responsibility and regulatory adherence, we are pleased to announce that our product lines are now fully compliant with the RoHS 3 Directive, EU 2015/863. This directive, which took effect on July 22, 2019, expands the list of hazardous substances restricted in electrical and electronic equipment. Compliance with these regulations is crucial for our continued access to the European market and reaffirms our dedication to sustainable practices.

The RoHS 3 Directive mandates the restriction of ten specific hazardous substances commonly used in the manufacture of electronic products. These substances include Lead (Pb), Mercury (Hg), Cadmium (Cd), Hexavalent chromium (Cr6+), Polybrominated biphenyls (PBB), Polybrominated diphenyl ethers (PBDE), Bis(2-ethylhexyl) phthalate (DEHP), Butyl benzyl phthalate (BBP), Dibutyl phthalate (DBP), and Diisobutyl phthalate (DIBP). Our compliance team has rigorously evaluated and tested our product models, including the ET-X200, ET-X300, ET-S100, and ET-P50, ensuring that all materials and components meet these stringent requirements. 

We have issued a Declaration of Conformity for each model, which certifies that our products comply with the RoHS 3 Directive. This declaration is available upon request and can be accessed through our internal compliance portal. These documents are essential for our distributors and partners who require assurance of our products' compliance with European regulations. 

The scope of products covered under the RoHS 3 Directive includes categories such as large household appliances, small household appliances, IT and telecommunications equipment, consumer equipment, lighting equipment, electrical and electronic tools, toys, leisure and sports equipment, and medical devices. ElectroTech’s product lines that fall within these categories have been assessed, and necessary adjustments have been made to align with the directive's stipulations.

For further inquiries or to obtain a copy of the Declaration of Conformity, please contact the Compliance Department at compliance@electrotech.com. Your cooperation in maintaining our compliance standards is greatly appreciated, and we thank you for your continued efforts towards making ElectroTech a leader in environmentally responsible electronics manufacturing. 

We remain committed to proactive engagement with regulatory changes and will continue to update our policies and practices to reflect evolving standards. The next policy revision is scheduled for review in June 2025. Please stay informed and participate in training sessions as they become available.
