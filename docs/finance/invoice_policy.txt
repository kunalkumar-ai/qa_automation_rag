To: All ElectroTech Staff

Date: January 15, 2025  
Subject: Updated Invoice Policy and Procedures  

As part of our ongoing commitment to maintaining high standards of financial accuracy and compliance, we are implementing a revised invoice policy effective February 1, 2025. This policy outlines the procedures regarding VAT treatment, the issuance of credit notes, processing invoice correction requests, and documentation retention periods. Compliance with these guidelines is mandatory for all departments.

**VAT Treatment:**  
ElectroTech adheres to the VAT Act 2020, Regulation No. 2025/12, which mandates that all invoices include a 20% VAT charge unless a valid exemption certificate is provided by the customer. Products such as the ET-X200 and ET-X300, which are exported outside the European Union, qualify for zero-rated VAT. Employees responsible for invoicing must ensure VAT numbers are verified and recorded accurately on all transactions. Any discrepancies must be reported to finance@electrotech.com for immediate resolution.

**Credit Note Process:**  
A credit note is issued to correct a previously issued invoice when there is a return of goods, an overcharge, or when an error is identified. Requests for credit notes must be submitted through the ElectroTech CRM system, referencing the original invoice number and providing detailed reasons for the issuance. All credit notes related to the ET-S100 or ET-P50 models must be approved by the department head before processing. The finance department will review and complete the issuance within three business days of approval.

**Invoice Correction Requests:**  
In the event of errors on issued invoices, a formal invoice correction request form must be submitted. This form, available on the company intranet, should be completed with precise details of the correction required, including the invoice number and the nature of the error. The updated policy revision number 2025/02 requires that all correction requests be processed within five business days to ensure prompt resolution and client satisfaction.

**Retention Period:**  
All financial documents, including invoices, credit notes, and correction requests, must be retained in our digital records management system for a period of seven years, in compliance with Regulation No. 2025/30. This retention period ensures that ElectroTech meets all statutory requirements for financial and audit purposes. Any documents older than seven years will be securely archived and subsequently destroyed.

For any queries or further clarification regarding this policy, please contact the finance department at finance@electrotech.com. Your adherence to these procedures is crucial in maintaining ElectroTech's reputation for excellence and regulatory compliance. 

Thank you for your cooperation.
