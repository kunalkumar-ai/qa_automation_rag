As part of our ongoing commitment to ensure compliance with international trade regulations and to streamline our shipping processes, ElectroTech is issuing this memorandum to outline the essential customs documentation required for our exports. This guidance specifically pertains to the shipment of our leading product lines, including the ET-X200, ET-X300, ET-S100, and ET-P50 models.

Firstly, the commercial invoice is a critical document serving as a bill of sale between the buyer and seller, and it is imperative that it meets the requirements set forth by international trade regulations. As of January 1, 2025, all commercial invoices issued by ElectroTech must include specific details: the complete description of the items, including model numbers (e.g., ET-X200), the quantity, unit price, and total price. It is also essential to include the country of origin, ElectroTech's tax identification number, and the invoice number. Please ensure that all commercial invoices are compliant with regulation number 2025/INV-R9.

In addition to the commercial invoice, the packing list is another crucial document required for customs clearance. It should detail all items included in the shipment, specifying the number of boxes, the weight, and dimensions of each box. The packing list must correlate directly with the commercial invoice to avoid discrepancies that could delay customs processing.

The certificate of origin is a document that certifies the country where the goods are manufactured. For all shipments of ET-X200, ET-X300, ET-S100, and ET-P50 products, the certificate must be prepared in accordance with regulation 2025/COR-12, which mandates that it includes the manufacturer's information, product details, and the authorized signature. This document is vital for determining tariffs and ensuring compliance with trade agreements.

Regarding incoterms, ElectroTech commonly utilizes Delivered Duty Paid (DDP) and Delivered At Place (DAP) terms. When using DDP, ElectroTech assumes responsibility for all shipping duties, taxes, and risks until the goods reach the buyer's specified location. Conversely, under DAP, the seller is responsible for delivering the goods to a designated place at the buyer's location, excluding import duty and taxes.

For further inquiries or guidance on preparing these documents, please contact the Compliance Department at compliance@electrotech.com. It is crucial that all departments adhere to these requirements to ensure seamless international shipping operations and maintain our reputation for excellence in the global market. Thank you for your attention to these important procedures.
