Subject: CE Marking Protocols for ElectroTech Products

As part of our ongoing commitment to regulatory compliance and market accessibility, this document outlines the necessary procedures and applicable directives regarding CE marking for our product lines, including models ET-X200, ET-X300, ET-S100, and ET-P50. Ensuring that our products meet the essential requirements of relevant European directives is crucial for maintaining our position in the European market.

1. **Applicable Directives**: The primary directives applicable to our products are the Low Voltage Directive (LVD) 2014/35/EU, the Electromagnetic Compatibility (EMC) Directive 2014/30/EU, and the Radio Equipment Directive (RED) 2014/53/EU. Each directive has specific requirements that must be met to achieve conformity.

   - **LVD** applies to electrical equipment operating with a voltage rating between 50 and 1000 V for alternating current and between 75 and 1500 V for direct current. This directive is relevant to our ET-X200 and ET-X300 models, which require rigorous testing to ensure they meet safety standards.
   
   - **EMC** is crucial for all our electronic products to ensure they do not emit electromagnetic interference that could affect other devices. The directive is particularly pertinent to our ET-S100 and ET-P50 models, which must demonstrate compliance through testing and technical documentation.
   
   - **RED** covers radio equipment and is applicable to any products with wireless communication capabilities. Our ET-P50 model, equipped with Bluetooth technology, must adhere to the requirements set forth in this directive.

2. **Notified Body**: For products requiring a higher level of assurance due to their complexity or use of radio frequencies, a Notified Body is engaged to conduct conformity assessments. Our designated Notified Body, NB 1234, will review our technical files and test results to confirm compliance. This step is especially critical for the ET-P50 model under the RED.

3. **Declaration of Conformity (DoC) Process**: The DoC is a crucial document asserting that our products comply with the applicable directives. It must be prepared and signed by an authorized representative of ElectroTech. The DoC for each product line should include the product description, applicable directives, standards used, the Notified Body details (if applicable), and the ElectroTech contact person. This document must be retained in our technical documentation and supplied to distributors and importers upon request.

All team members involved in product development and compliance should ensure familiarity with these directives and procedures. For further information or clarification, please contact the Regulatory Affairs Department at regulatory@electrotech.com.

This protocol remains subject to periodic review, with the next scheduled update in Revision 2.0, due December 2025. Compliance with these directives not only ensures legal conformity but also reinforces our commitment to safety and quality in our innovative product offerings.
