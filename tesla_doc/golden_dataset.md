# Tesla 10-K 2024 — Golden Dataset
# 20 questions with verified answers from the actual filing.
# Source line numbers refer to tsla-10k-2024.txt

---

## SECTION 1: BUSINESS (5 questions — Simple)

**Q1.** What are Tesla's two reportable business segments?
**A:** Automotive, and energy generation and storage.
**Source:** Line 111 — "We operate as two reportable segments: (i) automotive and (ii) energy generation and storage."

---

**Q2.** What is Tesla's stated mission?
**A:** To accelerate the world's transition to sustainable energy.
**Source:** Line 113 — "Our mission is to accelerate the world's transition to sustainable energy."

---

**Q3.** What five consumer vehicle models does Tesla currently manufacture?
**A:** Model 3, Model Y, Model S, Model X, and Cybertruck.
**Source:** Line ~130 — "We currently manufacture five different consumer vehicles – the Model 3, Y, S, X and Cybertruck."

---

**Q4.** What are Tesla's two main energy storage products and who are their target customers?
**A:** Powerwall (for homes and small commercial facilities) and Megapack (for commercial, industrial, utility and energy generation customers).
**Source:** Lines ~148-152 — "Powerwall...is designed to store energy at a home or small commercial facility. Megapack is an energy storage solution for commercial, industrial, utility and energy generation customers."

---

**Q5.** How many consumer vehicles did Tesla produce and deliver in 2024?
**A:** Produced approximately 1,773,000 and delivered approximately 1,789,000 consumer vehicles.
**Source:** Line ~1283 — "In 2024, we produced approximately 1,773,000 consumer vehicles and delivered approximately 1,789,000 consumer vehicles."

---

## SECTION 2: RISK FACTORS (7 questions — Medium)

**Q6.** What two specific events caused Tesla's production to decline in Q1 2024?
**A:** The early phase of the production ramp of the updated Model 3 at the Fremont factory, and factory shutdowns at Gigafactory Berlin-Brandenburg caused by shipping diversions from the Red Sea conflict and an arson attack.
**Source:** Line ~1345 — "we experienced a sequential decline in production volumes partially caused by the early phase of the production ramp of the updated Model 3 at our Fremont factory, and factory shutdowns at Gigafactory Berlin-Brandenburg resulting from shipping diversions caused by the Red Sea conflict and an arson attack."

---

**Q7.** In which locations does Tesla operate manufacturing facilities?
**A:** California, Nevada, Texas, China, and Germany. Mexico is mentioned as a future site.
**Source:** Line ~566 — "California, Nevada, Texas, China, Germany and any future sites such as Mexico."

---

**Q8.** What production risk did Tesla specifically flag for Q1 2025?
**A:** Simultaneous manufacturing ramps for the New Model Y in facilities on three continents, which may cause delays or declines in production volumes.
**Source:** Line ~1348 — "In the first quarter of 2025, as we launch our New Model Y worldwide, we may similarly experience delays or declines in production volumes due to simultaneous manufacturing ramps in facilities on three continents."

---

**Q9.** Which two previous vehicle models experienced launch delays due to supplier or manufacturing issues?
**A:** Model X (unanticipated supplier issues during initial ramp) and Model 3 (challenges with a supplier and ramping full automation in certain manufacturing processes).
**Source:** Lines ~557-560 — "we encountered unanticipated supplier issues that led to delays during the initial ramp of our first Model X and experienced challenges with a supplier and with ramping full automation for certain of our initial Model 3 manufacturing processes."

---

**Q10.** What macroeconomic factor did Tesla identify as impacting vehicle affordability?
**A:** Rising interest rates. Central banks raised interest rates rapidly in response to inflationary pressures, which impacted the affordability of vehicle lease and finance arrangements.
**Source:** Line ~1380 — "as inflationary pressures increased across the markets in which we operate, central banks in developed countries raised interest rates rapidly and substantially, which impacted the affordability of vehicle lease and finance arrangements."

---

**Q11.** What is Tesla's stated goal regarding delivery timing within each quarter?
**A:** Tesla is committed to reducing the percentage of vehicles delivered in the third month of each quarter, which will help reduce the cost per vehicle.
**Source:** Line ~1395 — "We are also committed to reducing the percentage of vehicles delivered in the third month of each quarter, which will help to reduce the cost per vehicle."

---

**Q12.** Why did Tesla say it needs to expand its Supercharger network, beyond serving its own customers?
**A:** Because other automotive manufacturers have announced adoption of the North American Charging Standard (NACS) and agreements with Tesla to use its Superchargers, creating additional demand on the network.
**Source:** Line ~1397 — "as other automotive manufacturers have announced their adoption of the North American Charging Standard ('NACS') and agreements with us to utilize our Superchargers, we must correspondingly expand our network."

## SECTION 3: MD&A (8 questions — Hard)

**Q13.** What was Tesla's total revenue in 2024, and how did it change compared to 2023?
**A:** Total revenue was $97.69 billion in 2024, an increase of $917 million (approximately 1%) compared to $96.77 billion in 2023.
**Source:** Revenue table line ~1490 — "Total revenues $97,690 $96,773... $917 1%"

---

**Q14.** Why did Tesla's net income decrease significantly from 2023 to 2024?
**A:** Net income was $7.09 billion in 2024, a decrease of $7.91 billion from the prior year. The primary reason was a one-time tax benefit in 2023 — Tesla released $6.54 billion of valuation allowance on U.S. federal and state deferred tax assets in Q4 2023, which inflated 2023 net income and did not repeat in 2024.
**Source:** Line ~1291 — "net income attributable to common stockholders was $7.09 billion, representing a decrease of $7.91 billion compared to the prior year, primarily due to the impact of releasing $6.54 billion of our valuation allowance."

---

**Q15.** What was Tesla's cash and investment position at the end of 2024?
**A:** $36.56 billion, an increase of $7.47 billion from the end of 2023.
**Source:** Line ~1294 — "We ended 2024 with $36.56 billion in cash and cash equivalents and investments, representing an increase of $7.47 billion from the end of 2023."

---

**Q16.** Why did automotive sales revenue decrease in 2024, and by how much?
**A:** Automotive sales revenue decreased by $6.03 billion (8%), from $78.51 billion to $72.48 billion. The primary causes were lower average selling prices due to price reductions and attractive financing offers, along with a decrease of approximately 22,000 combined Model 3 and Model Y cash deliveries.
**Source:** Lines ~1510-1515 — "Automotive sales revenue decreased $6.03 billion, or 8%...primarily due to lower average selling price on our vehicles driven by overall price reductions and attractive financing options...Additionally, there was a decrease of approximately 22,000 combined Model 3 and Model Y cash deliveries."

---

**Q17.** Why did energy generation and storage revenue increase in 2024, and by how much?
**A:** Revenue increased by $4.05 billion (67%), from $6.04 billion to $10.09 billion, primarily due to a 16.7 GWh increase in Megapack and Powerwall deployments compared to the prior year.
**Source:** Line ~1540 — "Energy generation and storage revenue increased $4.05 billion, or 67%...primarily due to a 16.7 GWh increase in Megapack and Powerwall deployments."

---

**Q18.** What drove the 54% increase in automotive regulatory credits revenue in 2024?
**A:** Demand for credits in North America increased because other automobile manufacturers are scaling back their battery electric vehicle plans, creating more demand for Tesla's credits.
**Source:** Line ~1520 — "driven by demand for credits in North America as other automobile manufacturers scale back on their battery electric vehicle plans."

---

**Q19.** What were Tesla's capital expenditures in 2024 and what does it expect going forward?
**A:** Capital expenditures were $11.34 billion in 2024 (up from $8.90 billion in 2023). Tesla expects capital expenditures to exceed $11.00 billion in 2025 and in each of the following two fiscal years.
**Source:** Lines ~1296-1297 and ~1430 — "Capital expenditures amounted to $11.34 billion in 2024...we currently expect our capital expenditures to exceed $11.00 billion in 2025 and in each of the following two fiscal years."

---

**Q20.** What specific factors drove the 27% increase in services and other revenue in 2024?
**A:** Increases in sales of used vehicles, non-warranty maintenance services and collision revenue, paid Supercharging revenue, insurance services revenue, and part sales revenue.
**Source:** Line ~1525 — "primarily due to increases in sales of used vehicles, non-warranty maintenance services and collision revenue, paid Supercharging revenue, insurance services revenue and part sales revenue."
