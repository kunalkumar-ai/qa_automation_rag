THESIS DISCUSSION NOTES
Date: 2026-05-30
=====================================================

CONTEXT
-------
Master thesis at a defense company.
Goal: Build a POC system to automate customer questionnaire responses.
University also requires a research contribution.

THE PROBLEM
-----------
- Defense company receives repetitive compliance questionnaires (e.g. "are you ISO certified?", "are you environment compliant?")
- Currently answered manually
- 500 real questionnaires already exist — questions + answers
- New/unknown questions should escalate to a human
- Auditability is a hard requirement (defense sector)
- Dual language: English and German (company requirement)

WHY NOT PLAIN RAG
-----------------
- Questions are static and policy-type — each maps to one answer
- A simple FAQ lookup outperforms RAG here
- RAG earns its complexity when answers require synthesizing across documents
- Key insight: copy-paste from approved source is safer than generation in defense
  (no hallucination risk, fully auditable, answer is exactly what a human approved)

THE ROUTING LOGIC (rough)
--------------------------
- Confidence >= 80%  → auto-answer (copy-paste from QnA database)
- Confidence 60-80%  → suggest 3 candidate answers, human picks and sends
- Confidence < 60%   → escalate directly to human

CORE PROBLEM WITH THIS APPROACH
--------------------------------
Setting thresholds at 60/80% is an engineering decision, not research.
The research question is: do similarity scores actually predict answer correctness?
A chunk can score 85% similar but be subtly wrong for the customer's specific context.
In defense, confidently wrong is worse than "I don't know."

CANDIDATE RESEARCH DIRECTIONS
------------------------------

Direction 1 — Confidence Calibration (STRONGEST)
  Research question: How do you calibrate confidence thresholds in an answer-selection
  system so they actually predict correctness — not just semantic similarity — in a
  high-stakes, auditable, bilingual setting?

  Key concept: Conformal Prediction — a statistical framework that gives rigorous,
  calibrated confidence measures with coverage guarantees (not just a guess).

  Gap: Most calibration papers assume the model *generates* an answer. Your system
  *selects* a pre-approved one. That's a meaningfully different problem.

  Papers to read:
  - ICLR 2025: Conformal Language Model Reasoning
    https://openreview.net/pdf?id=AJpUZd8Clb
  - QA-Calibration of LM Confidence Scores (2024)
    https://arxiv.org/pdf/2410.06615
  - Response Quality Assessment for RAG via Conformal Factuality
    https://arxiv.org/html/2506.20978
  - Bayesian RAG: Uncertainty-Aware Retrieval for Financial QA
    https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/artificial-intelligence/articles/10.3389/frai.2025.1668172/full

Direction 2 — Out-of-Distribution (OOD) Detection
  Research question: How do you reliably detect that an incoming question is genuinely
  outside your QnA database, not just a paraphrase of something you know?

  Why it matters: A false negative (system thinks it knows but it doesn't) is dangerous
  in defense. Current OOD methods may be answering the wrong question (2025 paper).

  Papers to read:
  - Out-of-Distribution Detection Methods Answer the Wrong Questions (2025)
    https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.01831
  - A Survey on Out-of-Distribution Detection in NLP
    https://arxiv.org/pdf/2305.03236

Direction 3 — Answer Attribution and Grounding
  Research question: How do you prove sentence-level provenance for every answer
  in a copy-paste QnA system in a regulated domain?

  Why it matters: Auditability = not just "we retrieved chunk X" but "this exact
  sentence in document Y version Z is the source." Auditors need this months later.

  Papers to read:
  - Model Internals-based Answer Attribution for Trustworthy RAG (2024)
    https://arxiv.org/pdf/2406.13663
  - Post-Hoc Answer Attribution for Long Document Comprehension (2024)
    https://arxiv.org/pdf/2406.06938

Direction 4 — System Comparison (most feasible for POC)
  Research question: Which approach (pure retrieval vs RAG vs answer selection)
  best balances accuracy + auditability for defense compliance questionnaires?

  Most feasible for a thesis that also needs a working system.
  Less publishable than Directions 1-3 but practically strong.

RECOMMENDED DIRECTION
---------------------
Direction 1 (calibration) + elements of Direction 2 (OOD/escalation) combined.
The bilingual (EN/DE) + defense/auditability context is the specific angle
that could make it novel even if calibration itself is studied.

EVALUATION PLAN
---------------
- Extract 500 real questionnaires (questions + answers)
- Build golden dataset: ~50-100 held-out questions with known correct answers
  across both EN and DE
- Metrics: accuracy, calibration error, escalation rate, auditability trace quality
- Split: training/validation set for threshold calibration, held-out test set for final eval

KEY INSIGHT FROM THIS CONVERSATION
------------------------------------
The RAG project (Tesla 10-K) was perfect preparation. You now understand
retrieval, chunking, reranking, and evaluation from the inside — including
WHY things fail (vocabulary gap, chunk dilution, wrong data source).

not a 473K char document), but harder in auditability and calibration requirements.
