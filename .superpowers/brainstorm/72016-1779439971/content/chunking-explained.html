How RAG Actually Works — From Document to Answer

Two phases: Ingestion (happens once) and Retrieval (happens every question)

PHASE 1 — Ingestion (done once when you set up the system)

Step 1: Chunking
Break the document into smaller pieces.
Why? Embeddings work poorly on very long text. LLMs have token limits. You need small pieces to find the right one.
Chunk 1: "Tesla's mission is to accelerate..."
Chunk 2: "We operate as two segments: automotive..."
Chunk 3: "Total revenues were $97.69 billion..."
Chunk 4: "Risk: supply chain disruptions may..."
... (hundreds more chunks)
🔢 Numbers
Each chunk becomes
1536 numbers
→
Step 2: Embedding
Convert each chunk into a list of numbers (a "vector"). Similar meaning = similar numbers.
Why numbers? You can't do math on words. But you CAN find the closest numbers to other numbers — that's how similarity search works.
"Tesla's mission..." → [0.12, -0.34, 0.87, 0.02, ...]
"revenue $97 billion..." → [0.45, 0.11, -0.22, 0.91, ...]
"supply chain risk..." → [0.33, -0.71, 0.15, -0.44, ...]
🗄️ ChromaDB
Stores all vectors
+ original text
→
Step 3: Store in Vector Database
ChromaDB stores each chunk's: original text + its vector + metadata (which section it came from).
Why store both? You search by vector (numbers) but you retrieve the original text to give to the LLM.

Step 4: Embed the Question
The question gets converted to a vector using the SAME embedding model.
Key insight: Questions and answers that mean similar things get similar vectors — even if the exact words are different.
"What was Tesla's revenue?" → [0.41, 0.09, -0.19, 0.88, ...]

Find closest
vectors

Step 5: Similarity Search in ChromaDB
ChromaDB finds the chunks whose vectors are CLOSEST to the question vector. "Closest" = most similar meaning.
This is where chunking quality matters most. If the revenue info was split across two chunks, neither chunk is a perfect match.
🤖 LLM
GPT-4o reads
chunks + answers
→
Step 6: Generate Answer
The top retrieved chunks + original question are sent to GPT-4o.
GPT-4o reads only those chunks (not the whole document) and generates an answer.
If the wrong chunks were retrieved, the answer will be wrong or hallucinated — no matter how smart the LLM is.

The key thing to remember

Retrieval quality limits everything. The LLM can only work with what it's given. A bad chunk = a bad answer, even with GPT-4o. This is why chunking strategy is the most important architectural decision.
