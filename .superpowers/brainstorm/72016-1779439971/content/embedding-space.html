How Embeddings Find Answers to Questions

And why this sometimes fails — the most important RAG concept to understand

What "vector space" actually means

Think of each embedding as a point in space. Texts with similar meaning are placed close together. The embedding model was trained on billions of sentences to learn this — it knows that "revenue" and "sales income" mean similar things, so they end up near each other.

Why questions and answers end up near each other

Embedding models like OpenAI's text-embedding-3-small were trained on massive datasets that include Q&A pairs. They learned that "What is the revenue?" and "Revenue was $97 billion" are semantically related — they capture the same concept from different angles.

The model doesn't match words. It matches meaning. "Revenue", "total sales", "income from operations" all land near each other in vector space.

But here's the problem — and why it sometimes fails

The question "What was Tesla's revenue?" and the chunk "Total revenues $97,690 million" are semantically close — but not identical. The embedding model bridges this gap most of the time.

Where it breaks: exact numbers, specific names, ticker symbols. If you ask "What was TSLA's FY24 top-line?" — financial jargon the model wasn't trained to associate with "revenue" — the embeddings drift apart and the right chunk might NOT be retrieved.

This is exactly why we add BM25 keyword search alongside embeddings. BM25 does exact word matching — it would find "revenue" and "2024" regardless of semantic drift. Hybrid search = semantic + keyword. Best of both.
